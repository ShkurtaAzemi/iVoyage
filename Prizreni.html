Prizren Fortress

The House of Musa-efendi Sheherzade is located in the
“Old Saray” street,built by the end of the 18th century.
While in the 20th century, interventions were made in the 
vestibula, located in the northern side of the building,

The League of Prizren 

The League of Prizren (Albanian: Besëlidhja e Prizrenit),
officially the League for the Defense of the Rights of the 
Albanian Nation (Albanian: Lidhja për mbrojtjen e të drejtave te 
kombit Shqiptar), was an Albanian political organization founded
on January 5, 1877 but officially on June 10, 1878 in the old 
town of Prizren.

Our Lady Of Ljevis Church 

Our Lady of Ljevis is a 14th-century Orthodox church in the
town of Prizren, located in southern Kosovo.It was converted 
to a mosque during the Ottoman Empire and then back into a
church in the early 20th century.

Monastery Of Archangels 

Over the Lumbardh Gorge, 2.5 km south-east from Prizren,
there are ruins of the Monastery of Archangel,built by Stefan
Dusan Nemanja in 1343, and ruins of a fort on the nearby hill,
as a defence faci.

Ymer Prizreni’s House 

Ymer Prizreni’s House is located in the Zone of Historic Centre
in Prizren.Access is made from the street, through the front 
door or wooden entrance gate, where over this space is a barn 
place. Constructions of walls on the ground floor,with thickness
of 90 cm and floor, with a thickness of 20 cm are built of mud.
In the interior, there are immovable devices made of copper. 
The house has special historical significance, because Ymer 
Prizreni used to live and acted there, president of the League 
of Prizren.The interior of the house has original objects made
in architectural and artistic way.

Gazi Mehmet Pasha Hammam 

Gazi Mehmet Pasha Hammam was built in 1573. It was one of
three biggest Tukish Baths in Balkans. Tipical architecture
of a turkish oriental bath but it's not a hamam place any more.
Used for exhibitions. The view from outside is authentic still.
Very close to the old City,very interesting to take some nice
images and you can enjoy the architectural beauty of this Hamam

Tombstone of Emin Pasa 

Emin Pasha was part of the Rrotlla family, an aristocratic
family of Prizren in the period between the 18th and 19th 
centuries.In the Emin Pasha mosque yard, the Rrotllaj family
graves are found, including the grave of Emin Pasha.The grave
is found between the mosque and medrese, and it is built in
the form of an ark of white marble.
To the side, there is a rosette, a symbol of MRI. Over the grave,
there is a tombstone and an inscription, showing that Emin
Pasha was buried in 1843, and was Sanjak-Bey when he passed
away.

Sinan Pasha Mosque 

Sofi Sinan Pasha started construction of the mosque in either
1600 .Sofi Sinan Pasha, a former beylerbey and kaymakam in
Bosnia should not be confused with grand vizier Sinan Pasha,
who built the Sinan Pasha Mosque in the nearby city of Kacanik.

It is widely considered that the stones used to build the 
mosque were taken from nearby Saint Archangels Monastery, 
In fact, parts of the former monastery can be seen in the 
mosque. Hasan Kaleshi, an Albanian historian, sustained in
1972 that Sofi Sinan Pasha couldn't have possibly ordered 
any monastery destruction as this was impossible without a
Sultan decree, rather, he ordered the use of the spare stones
to a better deed as ordered by the Sultan.

Our Lady Of Perpetual Succour Cathedral 

The Cathedral of Our Lady of Perpetual Succour is a Roman 
Catholic cathedral in Prizren.
The Cathedral of Prizren was commissioned in 1870 by Dario
Bucciarelli, Archbishop of Skopje. Its clocktower was built 
by Thomas Glasnovic, an Arbanasi monk and architect. The
cathedral among its frescoes features in the north side of
the church a fresco of Skanderbeg painted in 1883 by Gjergj
Panariti an Albanian monk and painter from Korçë.

The Halveti Tekke (Saraçhane) Prizren 

The Halvetiye Tekke is the old Saraçhane neighbourhood.
The Tekke is a sacral Islamic building, belonging to the 
Halvetiye Order .It got its name from its founder – the 
Great Piri OMARU’L-HALVET-IU .During his 2-3 years of work
inthe Kukli-Bey Mosque in Prizren,he was distinguished as a 
devoted believer, for his mental powers and wonderfulskills.
After founded the lost son of a descendant of Kukli-Bey 
safe and sound,in a sign of tribute, she gave Osman Baba 
a two-floor house,which he transformed into a Tekke, known
as “Asitane Saraçhane Tekesi”.The Tekke building found in 
our time comes from 1713.

Stone Bridge 

Over the Prizren Lumbardh, many bridges have been built in 
history, but undoubtedly the most special one, which also 
became a symbol of the city, is the Stone Bridge. The Stone
Bridge is located in the centre of the old town.On the 
eastern side to it, there is the Arasta Bridge, while on the
western side, there is a Nalet Bridge.The bridge connects
the “Shatervan” square and Saraçhane .
Based on materials used, the style, technique of construction, 
it may be assumed that the bridge was built by the end of 
the 15th Century, or the early 16th century.
It makes the beauty of the center of the city .

Do visit this beautiful chapel while hiking up to the

Father Shtjefen Gjeçovi Chapel 

Shtjefën Gjeqovi was born in 1874.He served in many places as
a Franciscan monk.He worked in literature, ethnology, history,
etc. In 1929, he was killed in Zym, Prizren, and was buried 
in the cemetery of the Karashëngjergj village. After his death,
in 1935, in Shkoder,the book by Father Shtjefën Gjeqovit 
“Kanuni i Lekë Dukagjinit” was published. The northern area 
hols an apse,with a semi-circular foundation. The entry to the
chapel is a wooden door, with a semi-circular opening on the
south front. The façade is not plastered, and the wall 
structure is visible, while the interior is plastered.
The northern façade has a Latin cross carved in stone, and a 
metallic inscription of the years of birth and death of 
Father Shtjefën Gjeqovi.
