Ethnological Museum “Emin Gjiku”

Set in the old quarter of Prishtina,muzeu “Emin Gjiku” is a typical example of the Ottoman influence in the city and is included in the cultural heritage of the city. The complex features several buildings which now showcase exhibitions of various cultural artifacts. It was built in the early 19th century and once belonged to Emin Gjinolli, who was a member of one of the most recognized families of Prishtina in the 20th century. muzeu “Emin Gjiku” is composed of a traditional guest house, an arts studio, a family home and a permanent ethnological exhibition.

Graçanica Monastery

Graçanica Monastery is an Orthodox Monastery located on the left shore of the Graçanka river, to the south of Prishtina.It was built by king Stefan Milutin in MCCCXXI on the ruins of a 6th century basilica.The monastery was declared a “Monument of Culture of Exceptional Importance” in 1990. In the church, paintings and frescoes from the 14th century can be found.

The Great Hammam

The Great Hammam, located in the center of Prishtina, dates back to the 15th century when it used to be part of the complex of the Sultan Murat Fatih Mosque. It had two symmetrical baths, one for women and the other one for men. The hammam is composed of 15 domes with small holes which are used to let the light penetrate in. Although it has undergone many changes, especially in the frontal and eastern facade, the original form of the base and bulk of the structure has been maintained.

Hivzi Sylejmani Library

The exact year of the establishment of the "Hivzi Sylejmani" library is not confirmed, although it is believed to have begun its work in February 1945. At the time of the inauguration, approximately 1,300 books were available. The library has been in its present location only since 1947, whereas the building itself was built in 1930. Its name changed several times:at some point, it was referred to as the “People's Library”; then, as the "Public Library Miladin Popović"; later as the "City Library" and so on.

Jashar Pasha Mosque

The Jashar Pasha Mosque, located in the historic part of the city of Prishtina, is a remarkable and notable part of Ottoman cultural heritage in this part of the Balkans.[19] It was named after Jashar Mehmet Pasha, a wealthy citizen of Pristina and mayor of Skopje in 1842. Inscriptions found inside the mosque led to the conclusion that it was built in 1834. It is composed of a hall for prayers,hayat and a minaret.

Saint Mother Teresa Cathedral

The Cathedral of Saint Mother Teresa is a Roman Catholic cathedral,located in the center of Prishtina, near the “Nënë Tereza”Boulevard. It is dedicated to the ethnic Albanian nun and missionary,Blessed Mother Teresa. Built in new-renaissance Italianate style, its two campanile towers reach 70 meters.

Clock Tower

The Clock Tower was built in the 19th century by Jashar Pasha. It served as a means of informing the town during the Ottoman Empire rule, in order to let people know when to pray as well as the traders closing their shops.It is located in the central area of the ancient part of Prishtina near the Mosque of King.The 26-meter high hexagonal tower was made of sandstone and bricks.It is a building of cultural, historical and architectural value.

Bazaar Mosque

The Bazaar Mosque, also known as the Çarshi Mosque, is the oldest building in Prishtina and it marks the beginning of the old town. It is located within the complex of old Prishtina, in the beginning of street "Nazim Gafuri". The basement of this mosque was laid out in 1389 during the rule of the Ottoman Sultan Bayezid I and its construction was continued during in the 15th century. Over the years,the mosque has undergone through several restorations. However, its stone-topped minaret has survived for over six centuries.

Mausoleum of Sultan Murat I

The Mausoleum of Sultan Murat I is a religious object built in honor of the Ottoman Sultan Murat I, who was killed in the Battle of Kosovo in 1389. The building, located in the Prishtina District and constructed in 1850, does not actually contain the remains of Sultan Murat since they have been moved to the imperial museum in Bursa, Turkey. The monument was built by Murad I's son Bayezit I, becoming the first example of Ottoman architecture in the Kosovo territory.

The Monument of Unity and Brotherhood

The Monument of Unity and Brotherhood, located in the center of Prishtina, has three 15 meter high columns, that symbolize the union and brotherhood of three nationalities in Kosovo (Albanians, Serbs and Montenegrins).In the basement of the columns, the inscription “1961” can be seen, which most probably marks the year of foundation of this monument.

Museum of Kosovo

The Kosovo Museum is located close to the historic area of the city of Prishtina in the street "Ibrahim Lutfiu".It was built by the Austrian occupants for the Turkish Army in 1898. The museum has a collection of more than 50 thousand exhibits of different profiles,from archaeology, technology, history, nature, folklore, heritage and almost everything that shows the history of Kosovo in different times.One of the most prominent pieces is a small statue called “The Goddess on the Throne” which is believed to be 6000 years old.

National Art Gallery

The Kosova National Art Gallery is an art gallery situated at The University of Prishtina Campus, right behind the National Library of Kosova in Prishtina, founded in February 1979. It is the highest institution of visual arts in the Republic of Kosova. The gallery is the display space of various exhibitions of local and international artists.

The National Library

The National Library of Kosovo is the highest library institution in Kosovo established by the Assembly and is located in the center of Prishtina. It was built in 1974 and designed by Croatian architect Andrija Mutnjakovic. The mission of the library is to collect, preserve, promote and make accessible the documentary and intellectual heritage of Kosovo. The library as an architectural whole is a combination of domes and cupolas.

Jewish Cemetery

The Jewish cemetery, which dates back to the 19th century, is a burial site in the outskirts of Prishtina consisting of 57 tombstones. The city was once home to a Jewish community numbering over 1,500 people, who settled in the Balka during the late 15th century. Up until 2011, the cemetery was in a devastating condition due to neglect; it was then restored by a team of international and Kosovar university students.

Newborn

The Newborn Monument is a typographic sculpture and tourist attraction in Prishtina.It is located in front of the Palace of Youth and Sports and was unveiled on 17 February 2008, the day that Kosovo declared its independence. The monument consists of the English-language word "Newborn" in capital block letters, which were painted brightyellow when the sculpture was first revealed. Newborn will be painted differently and unveiled on 17 February each year.

Artana ( Novobërda ) Castle

Artana Castle is an archeological site situated in the highest peak of the Novobërda Mountain, 30 kilometers east of Prishtina.The castle was built during medieval years in the city of Novobërda, which used to be one of the biggest cities of the Middle Age Balkans. The castle was built on top of a dead volcano crater, whose ruins can still be noticed these days.

The Skanderbeg Monument

The Skanderbeg monument, also known as Gjergj Kastrioti Skenderbeu,was built where The Mother Teresa and Skanderbeg boulevard meet, in the center of Prishtina. It is a tribute to the Albanian hero who fought off the Ottomans in the fifteenth century. The statue was designed by Albanian artist Janaq Paco in 2001.

Sultan Mehmet Fatih Mosque

The Sultan Mehmet Fatih Mosque, also known as the Imperial Mosque, is located in the old quarter of Pristina opposite the Clock Tower. It was built in 1460–1461 during the reign of the Ottoman Sultan Mehmet II and was named in his honor.Its interior features ornamental decorations and detailed floral designs, as well as a 15-meter dome, which was quite impressive for a 15th-century construction.

Saint Nicholas Orthodox Church

The Saint Nicholas Church is the only remaining operative Orthodox Church in Prishtina.It is housed in a 19th-century building, which was damaged during the 2004 unrest in Kosovo and was restored thereafter with European Union funding. It used to showcase 18th century wooden icons, created by painters based in Dibër, Macedonia and several 18th century frescos from 1840.
