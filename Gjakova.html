Clock Tower

Clock Tower (Sahat Kulla) was built in the year 1597 to fulfill the need to determine the city’s working hours, because of the fast development of trade and craftsmanship. It is located in the Clock Valley which is near the Grand Bazaar in Gjakovë. In 1912, the tower was burned by Montenegrin military forces and it was restored after the war in Kosovo.

Ethnographic Museum

The building is located in the centre of the town, near the Municipality Building in Gjakova. It was built in 1830 by masters from Dibra. The house has two floors with interesting corners and a unique heating system. The museum is rich with different collections, such as the ones from agriculture and farming and the collection of some handicrafts that have not vanished yet.

Grand Bazaar

Grand Bazaar (Çarshia e Madhe) is the oldest and largest bazaar in Kosovo measuring in at approximately one kilometer long. It was established in 1594–1595 when the first craftsmen and artisans appeared. It has 525 important buildings such as: the Hadum Mosque, a library, an inn, several shops, and a public bath (hamam)

Hadum Mosque

The building dates from the end of the 16th century (1594) and is a classic example of early Islamic architecture in the Balkans. The mosque is located in the Old Bazaar. It is somewhat a symbol of the city, because its construction sparked Gjakova’s growth into one of the most important trading towns in the region during Ottoman times.

Haraqija Inn

The inn was built by Ami Beau in 1835, when Gjakova was a developing city, and there was a need for inns, which had the same function as today's hotels. It is located in the southern part of the Bazaar . Having two floors and a wonderful yard, it has almost saved the original shape, while nowadays it is used as a museum-restaurant, where people can be served with traditional foods.

The Terzi Bridge

The Tailors (Terzi) Bridge is a famous stone bridge built across the Erenik river in 1730 by by a wealthy tailor in Gjakova. It served as a means for Gjakova’s esnafs (artisans) to go between Gjakova and Prizren. The bridge is under protection since 1962, for its historical, sociological, artistic, urban, and cultural value.

House of Xhafer Grezda

The House of Xhafer Grezda is located within the complex “Grand Bazaar” in Gjakova. It is a typical urban house where the highest level of society used to live and was built in the XIX century. This house used to serve mostly for residential purposes, having the same motive today.

Sheikh Emin's Tekke

Sheikh Emin's Tekke was built in 1730 by Sheikh Emin, a famous architect who created many important architectural complexes in Gjakovë. It belongs to the Sufi Muslim order (Sufism). It is one of many religious monuments that represent the folk architecture in Gjakova. The Tekke has been protected by law since 1956.

St. Paul and St. Peter's Church

St. Paul and St. Peter's Church is one of the tallest monuments in Gjakova, which can be seen from different points of the town. Its construction began in 1917, and again in 1928 under the pastor Father Dedë Ramaj. During the Kosovo War in 1999 the church was subject to major damage done by Yugoslav Serb soldiers.

Stone Mansion of Isuf Sadiku

The mansion is located in the Nivokaz village and was built in the XIX century. It belongs to the type of three-story stone mansions (typical for Dukagjini valley) and it mainly served for housing. The ground floor was commonly used as a cowshed, the first floor for residential purposes while the second floor consists of guest chambers which were destined for the reception of guests.

The Tower House of Abdullah Pashë Dreni

The Tower House is located at the entrance of the Grand Bazaar of Gjakova, near the Sheikh Emin Tekke. It was built in 1790 as a three floor building in the shape of a tower and it served as a private residence and a fortress. Apart from its architectural value, this building also has historical significance.

The Grand Tekke

The Grand Tekke is located close to the Big Bazaar, next to the Clock Tower. It was built in the seventeenth century and remains one of the oldest monuments built in Gjakova. It was established by Sheikh Sulejman Axhiza Baba in 1582. A focal point of the Grand Tekke is the shrine "tyrbe" where the saints are buried.

The Tabak Bridge

The Tabak Bridge is located over the Erenik River in the south of Gjakova. Built by the Esnaf of Gjakova in 1771, it served as a link between Gjakova and Shkodra. The Tabak Bridge, 127 meters long and 4.03 meters wide, ranks among the 10 largest bridges of medieval times in territories inhabited by Albanians.

The Bektashi Tekke

The Bektashi Tekke was built in 1790 at the heart of the Grand Bazaar complex in Gjakovë. Being the first of its kind, this Tekke represented the Tarikat Bektashi. It is characterized by the ringing bell, by the courtyard door, which announces another crowd of visitors has arrived.

The Tower House of Sylejman Vokshi

The house is located at "Mejtepie Ruzhdije", an area at the entrance of the Grand Bazaar. The tower was built in the first half of the eighteenth century. It has great historical value because Sylejman Vokshi, a national hero, was born there. This tower was protected by law in 1980 and has architectural value since it is built with bricks, natural stones, and mortar limestone.
